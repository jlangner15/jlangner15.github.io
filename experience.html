I specifically developed the module that utilized SPI communication. This project followed a rigorous software design process that included:

  • Design specification
  • Software Engineering principals
  • Design strategy and implementation
  • Code review and testing
  • Documentation

Engineering Competition Finalists

McMaster Engineering Competition 2020

The McMaster Engineering Competition(MEC) 2020 was run as a 2 day virtual event for all undergraduate engineering students who wanted to participate. This years competition was significantly different since it was entirely virtual. The goal was for each member to create a Rudy Goldberg machine, film it performing a simple task in an entertaining yet effective manor. These videos were first combined in a slideshow and shown to the judges. Five groups, including us, moved on to the next stage where we had to pitch our ideas. Our pitch involved presenting to the panel of judges with while presenting the informal ideas of the machine in a technical and formal format. Although this experience was different than the previous year it was a great opportunity to practice and present over virtual meetings and collaborate with members virtually as well.

McMaster Engineering Competition 2019

The MEC in 2019 was a more traditional style since it was an in person single day event. The task was to create a freestanding structure to withstand a certain amount of wind from a single source. Our group of four was given several specifications and constraints regarding our solution. The two biggest constraints were time and cost. We had to plan, build and prototype a our structure using materials that we had to "purchase" from the committee using the given budget. We also had deadlines on presenting, demonstrating and submitting all work involved. Overall this was a great experience to improve collaborating and problem solving skills since it was with an unfamiliar group with a small scale problem but realistic constraints and expectations.
